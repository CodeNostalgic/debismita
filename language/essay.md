### Screens Are Killing Children's Imagination

In today's digital world, screens have become an inseparable part of children's lives. From online classes and educational videos to games and social media, children spend several hours every day staring at phones, tablets, computers, or televisions. While technology has undoubtedly made learning easier and more accessible, I strongly believe that excessive screen time is slowly diminishing children's imagination and creativity.

Imagination flourishes when children explore, observe, question, and create. In the past, children spent their evenings reading storybooks, building toy houses, drawing pictures, inventing games, or simply playing outdoors with friends. A cardboard box could become a spaceship, and a stick could transform into a magical sword. Such activities encouraged them to think independently and develop original ideas.

Today, however, screens provide ready-made images, stories, and solutions. Instead of imagining how a dragon might look, children merely watch animated dragons on a screen. Video games and short videos entertain them instantly, leaving little room for creative thinking. Since every scene is presented visually, the brain has fewer opportunities to construct its own images or invent new possibilities.

Moreover, excessive screen time often reduces meaningful social interaction. Children who spend hours on digital devices may miss conversations with family members, outdoor adventures, and group activities that teach cooperation, empathy, and problem-solving. These real-life experiences play a vital role in shaping a child's imagination.

This does not mean that technology is harmful in itself. Educational apps, documentaries, coding platforms, and digital art tools can inspire creativity when used wisely. The real problem arises when entertainment dominates learning and screen time replaces books, hobbies, sports, and imaginative play.

In conclusion, screens are wonderful servants but poor masters. Parents and teachers must encourage children to strike a healthy balance between technology and traditional activities. A child's imagination is like a young plant—it grows best when it receives the sunshine of curiosity, the water of experience, and the freedom to dream beyond the limits of a screen.


## Grades Do Not Define a Student's Intelligence

In almost every school, examination results are eagerly awaited by students, parents, and teachers. High grades are often celebrated as a sign of intelligence, while poor marks are viewed as failure. However, I strongly disagree with the statement that grades define a student's intelligence. Although grades measure academic performance to some extent, they cannot fully reflect a person's abilities, creativity, or potential.

Firstly, intelligence is a broad concept. Some students excel in mathematics and science, while others possess remarkable talent in music, art, sports, writing, or public speaking. A student who struggles with algebra may compose beautiful poems or play a musical instrument with extraordinary skill. Such talents cannot be measured through written examinations alone.

Secondly, examinations test performance on a particular day under specific conditions. A student may perform poorly due to illness, stress, anxiety, or personal problems despite having studied sincerely. Similarly, some students are excellent at memorising facts and scoring high marks but may find it difficult to apply their knowledge in real-life situations. Therefore, grades reveal only a part of a student's capabilities.

History provides numerous examples of successful people who were not outstanding in school. Many inventors, entrepreneurs, artists, and athletes achieved greatness through perseverance, creativity, and determination rather than exceptional report cards. Their achievements remind us that intelligence is not confined to examination halls.

This does not mean that grades are unimportant. They encourage discipline, regular study habits, and healthy competition. Good grades also open doors to higher education and scholarships. Nevertheless, they should be regarded as indicators of academic progress rather than the sole measure of intelligence.

In conclusion, every child is unique and possesses different strengths. True education should nurture curiosity, critical thinking, kindness, creativity, and resilience alongside academic excellence. Grades may reflect how well a student performs in examinations, but they can never define the depth of a person's intelligence or determine the success that awaits them in life.

## Technological Evolution Has Made Students Lazy Thinkers

The rapid advancement of technology has transformed the way students learn. The internet, artificial intelligence, educational apps, and digital libraries have placed an ocean of knowledge at our fingertips. While these innovations have made education more accessible, I believe that excessive dependence on technology is gradually making many students lazy thinkers.

Earlier, students visited libraries, referred to several books, and spent time analysing information before arriving at a conclusion. Today, a simple search engine or an AI tool can provide instant answers within seconds. Although this saves time, it often discourages students from questioning, analysing, and thinking independently. Many simply copy information without understanding the concepts behind it.

Another concern is the decline in problem-solving skills. Whether it is solving mathematical equations, writing essays, or completing projects, students often rely on calculators, online solutions, or AI-generated responses. As a result, they may finish their work quickly but fail to develop logical reasoning and creativity. The habit of finding instant answers weakens the ability to think deeply and solve unfamiliar problems.

Technology has also reduced the attention span of many students. Short videos, instant notifications, and endless scrolling encourage the brain to seek quick entertainment rather than sustained concentration. Reading a lengthy chapter or solving a challenging problem now seems difficult for many learners because they have become accustomed to instant gratification.

However, technology itself is not the enemy. When used wisely, it can inspire curiosity and innovation. Virtual laboratories, educational videos, coding platforms, and online courses have enabled millions of students to learn beyond the boundaries of the classroom. The real issue lies in how technology is used. It should assist thinking, not replace it.

In conclusion, technological evolution has brought remarkable opportunities, but students must avoid becoming dependent on it for every answer. True education develops the ability to question, reason, imagine, and create. Technology should be a powerful tool in the hands of thoughtful learners, not a shortcut that weakens the habit of independent thinking. A balanced approach will ensure that students remain both technologically skilled and intellectually strong.

Since then, I have learnt to plan my time better and never allow urgency to overcome good judgement. Whenever I see a red traffic signal today, I no longer think of it as an obstacle. Instead, I see it as a silent reminder that every life is precious and every responsible choice matters.

My grandmother has always believed that a garden is more than a collection of plants—it is a place where memories quietly take root. During my summer vacation last year, I discovered how true her words were.

One pleasant evening, I was helping my grandmother remove weeds from a flower bed. As my small spade struck something hard beneath the soil, I assumed it was a stone. Although I had never met him, his words made me feel as though I were listening to his voice across the years.

That little key unlocked far more than an old wooden chest. It opened a door to my family's history and reminded me that the greatest treasures are often memories rather than material possessions. Even today, whenever I visit my grandmother's garden, I remember that beneath ordinary soil, extraordinary stories sometimes lie waiting to be discovered.


## A Scorching Summer Day

Last May, I experienced one of the hottest days I can remember. Even before the sun had risen completely, the air felt warm and heavy. By mid-morning, the bright blue sky had turned into a blazing dome of fire, and the scorching sun seemed determined to drain every drop of energy from the earth.

Stepping outside was like walking into an oven. The roads shimmered in the intense heat, creating wavering illusions that looked like pools of water. A hot, dusty wind blew through the streets, carrying dry leaves and fine sand with it. The trees stood motionless, their leaves drooping as though exhausted. The fragrance of blooming flowers had disappeared, replaced by the smell of hot dust and heated concrete.

The neighbourhood, usually filled with children's laughter, was unusually quiet. Shops remained half-empty, and only a few people ventured outdoors, shielding themselves with umbrellas, scarves, or hats. The occasional sound of an ice cream vendor's bell echoed through the silent streets, bringing brief excitement to children peeping from their windows.

The intense heat affected birds and animals the most. Sparrows perched silently in the shade with their beaks open, panting for relief. Crows gathered around a small earthen bowl of water that my mother had placed on our balcony. Stray dogs rested beneath parked vehicles, while cows searched for shelter under the few remaining trees. Even the playful squirrels, usually darting across branches, appeared sluggish and inactive.

Inside our house, ceiling fans seemed to circulate only warm air, and even chilled water offered temporary relief. Everyone avoided unnecessary work and waited impatiently for evening. At last, dark clouds gathered on the horizon, and a gentle breeze began to blow. Although it did not rain that day, the cooler wind brought immense comfort and renewed hope.

That sweltering day reminded me that rising temperatures are no longer an occasional inconvenience but a warning about our changing climate. Protecting trees, conserving water, and caring for birds and animals are responsibilities we all share. Nature speaks softly at first, but if we ignore its signs, the consequences may become impossible to overlook.


## Where There Is a Will, There Is a Way

The small village of Devpur had only one government school. Among its students was fourteen-year-old Aarav, whose greatest dream was to become an engineer. However, life was far from easy. Since frequent power cuts made it difficult to read at night, he often sat beneath the only street lamp in the village with his books spread across an old wooden bench.

One day, the school announced a state-level science exhibition. Aarav wished to participate but had no money to buy materials. Refusing to give up, he collected discarded cardboard boxes, plastic bottles, broken wires, and old batteries from nearby shops. Using these simple materials, he built a working model of an irrigation system powered by solar energy.

On the day of the exhibition, his model attracted the attention of the judges because of its practical design and low cost. Aarav explained how farmers could use it to conserve water and reduce electricity expenses. Impressed by his creativity and confidence, the judges awarded him the first prize. More importantly, a charitable organisation offered him a scholarship that covered his education until college.

Years later, Aarav returned to his village as a successful engineer. Standing before the students of his old school, he smiled as he pointed towards the same street lamp under which he had once studied.

"I did not succeed because I was the smartest," he said. "I succeeded because I refused to stop trying."

His words echoed through the school grounds, reminding everyone that obstacles may slow determined people, but they can never defeat them. Truly, where there is a will, there is always a way.


## The Forgotten Station

"They said the train hadn't stopped at that station in decades—until it did, just as I arrived."

I looked around in disbelief. The tiny platform was deserted except for an old station building with faded walls and a rusted signboard that read **Shantipur Halt**. Wild grass had grown between the railway tracks, and the silence was broken only by the chirping of birds.

I had been travelling to my cousin's village when heavy rain forced our bus to take a diversion. While searching for shelter, I had unknowingly wandered to this abandoned station. Just as I wondered how I would continue my journey, the distant whistle of a train echoed through the valley.

An elderly man sitting on a wooden bench smiled mysteriously. "It hasn't stopped here for nearly forty years," he said, "but today seems different."

To my astonishment, the train slowed down and came to a complete halt. The guard stepped out and announced that a fallen tree further along the track had forced the train to stop temporarily until the line was cleared.

While the passengers stretched their legs, I noticed a weathered photograph hanging inside the old stationmaster's office. It showed a crowded platform decorated with flowers and flags. Curious, I asked the elderly man about it.

He sighed and began his story. Years ago, Shantipur Halt had been a busy station that connected several villages. However, after a new railway line was built, trains stopped coming, and the station was gradually abandoned. "This place once echoed with laughter, farewells, and joyful reunions," he said softly. "Now, only memories remain."

Listening to him, I realised how easily time can erase places that were once full of life.

An hour later, the track was cleared, and the train was ready to leave. Before boarding, I turned to thank the old man, but the bench was empty. A railway worker nearby smiled and said, "People often say an old stationmaster loved this place so much that he never truly left it."

As the train pulled away, I looked back at the lonely platform growing smaller in the distance. Whether the old man had been real or merely a keeper of forgotten memories, I could not say. But I knew one thing for certain: some places may disappear from maps, yet they continue to live in the stories and hearts of those who remember them.

As I walked home that evening, I realised that appearances can often be misleading. What had seemed like a theft was, in reality, the desperate act of a lonely man clinging to the happiest chapter of his life. "Can we talk?" she asked softly.

He nodded.

Taking a deep breath, Ananya said, "My painting is missing. Some students told me they saw you with it. I don't want to accuse you unfairly, but I need to know the truth."

For a few moments, Rohan remained silent. Then he slowly opened his school bag and took out the missing painting.

"I was going to return it," he whispered. "I never meant to steal it."

Confused, Ananya listened as he explained.

Rohan's younger sister had always admired Ananya's artwork. She had recently been admitted to hospital with a serious illness, and before leaving home she had told him how much she wished she could see the painting that everyone in school was talking about. Unable to take her to the exhibition, Rohan had foolishly borrowed the painting without asking, hoping to show it to her after school and return it the next morning.

"I knew it was wrong," he admitted, tears filling his eyes. "I just didn't know how to tell anyone."

Ananya remained silent for a moment. She was upset, but she could also see the guilt on his face.

Together, they informed the art teacher about everything. Rohan apologised sincerely and accepted the punishment of missing the exhibition as a participant. However, the teacher appreciated his honesty and allowed him to volunteer during the event instead.

A week later, Ananya and a few classmates visited Rohan's sister in hospital with paintings and handmade greeting cards. Her smile was brighter than any prize they could have won.

As Ananya reflected on the incident, she realised that confronting someone with courage was important, but doing so with kindness was even more valuable. Sometimes, understanding another person's reasons can transform anger into compassion and mistakes into opportunities for forgiveness.


## A Dream in Print

*"As Prerna turned the first page of the newspaper, her eyes fell on an announcement..."*

**"National Young Innovators Challenge – Entries Invited from School Students. The winning project will receive a scholarship and an opportunity to present before leading scientists."**

Prerna read the notice twice. Ever since childhood, she had loved building simple machines from discarded cardboard, plastic bottles, and old electronic parts. Yet she had never imagined that her ideas could compete on a national stage.

She carefully cut out the announcement and pinned it above her study table. The deadline was only four weeks away.

Every evening after school, Prerna worked tirelessly on her project—a low-cost automatic watering system for small kitchen gardens. She collected used bottles, old pipes, a tiny water pump, and a moisture sensor donated by her science teacher. The first model leaked badly. The second stopped working after a few minutes. More than once, she felt like giving up.

Seeing her disappointment, her grandmother smiled and said, "Every invention has a history of failures hidden behind its success."

Encouraged by those words, Prerna kept improving her design. After several attempts, the system finally worked. It watered the plants only when the soil became dry, helping to conserve water.

On the day of the competition, hundreds of students displayed remarkable inventions. Looking around at the sophisticated models, Prerna felt nervous. However, when the judges reached her stall, she confidently demonstrated her project and explained how it could help families with small gardens save water during the hot summer months.

Weeks later, the results were published.

With trembling hands, Prerna unfolded the morning newspaper. There, under the headline **"Young Innovators Honoured,"** she saw her own photograph smiling back at her. She had won the first prize.

As reporters surrounded her for interviews, Prerna quietly remembered the small newspaper announcement that had started everything. A single paragraph, read over breakfast, had changed the course of her life.

That day she realised that opportunities often arrive silently, hidden among the ordinary pages of everyday life. Those who recognise them, prepare diligently, and persevere despite failure are the ones who transform simple dreams into extraordinary achievements.


## The Silent Promise

*"The old bridge groaned under the weight of the passing cart, but its creaking was no match for the silence of the surrounding forest."*

I was walking home from school, taking the long route through the woods just to enjoy the evening breeze. The path led past the ancient stone bridge that spanned the shallow river—a landmark my grandfather used to say had stood for more than a hundred years.

As I approached the bridge, I noticed something glinting near the edge. It was a small, worn leather diary. I picked it up carefully. The pages were yellowed and damp with humidity.

Most of the writing inside was in an elegant, looping script I barely recognised. But on the last few pages, the ink was faded and smudged. I could make out a date from many years ago—the exact date of my grandfather's birthday, which was tomorrow.

Then I found a loose note tucked inside. It read: *"To my dearest grandchild, when you cross this bridge on your tenth birthday, look closely at the third stone. I have left something there for you."*

My heart skipped a beat. I was turning ten tomorrow. I had never heard my grandfather mention any promise or any hidden gift.

I carefully opened the diary to the page dated seventy years earlier. It contained simple, loving entries about my grandmother, his garden, and his dreams for the future. He had written about how he hoped his grandchildren would always remember the quiet beauty of the place they called home.

Unable to wait, I gently pushed aside the moss on the third stone from the start of the bridge. Beneath it, wrapped in an old piece of cloth, was a small silver locket. It was engraved with the initials 'A.K.'—my grandmother's name.

I sat on the riverbank, clutching the locket. I realised that my grandfather had kept his promise for decades, waiting patiently for the right moment to share this piece of his past. He had passed away years before I was born, yet here was a message speaking directly to me from his youth.

As the sun set, casting golden light over the water, I understood that some promises are not spoken aloud but are kept through time and memory. That evening, I learned that love can travel across generations, and that sometimes, the most meaningful gifts are found not in treasure chests, but in forgotten stories waiting to be discovered.

## When the Seeds Refused to Grow

Last spring, I carefully prepared a small patch of soil in my garden and sowed a handful of sunflower seeds. Every morning I watered them, loosened the soil, and eagerly searched for the first tiny shoots. Days passed, then weeks, but nothing appeared. Disappointed, I almost gave up.

One moonlit night, I stepped into the garden and was startled to hear faint whispers coming from beneath the soil.

"We don't want to grow," a tiny voice said.

I looked around in surprise.

"Who's there?"

"We are the seeds," came the reply. "Why should we sprout into a world where forests are cut down, rivers are polluted, and the air grows dirtier every day? Humans expect us to provide shade, flowers, fruits, and oxygen, yet they destroy the very earth that gives us life."

For a moment, I had no answer. Their complaint was painfully true.

After gathering my thoughts, I knelt beside the flower bed and said gently, "Not every human has forgotten nature. There are children who plant saplings every year, volunteers who clean rivers, scientists developing cleaner energy, and ordinary families who place bowls of water for thirsty birds during summer. Every tree standing today exists because someone believed it was worth planting."

The garden fell silent.

I continued, "If you refuse to grow, the world will lose another chance to become greener. But if you bloom, you will inspire people to protect nature. Change begins with a single seed, just as a forest begins with a single tree."

The whispers faded.

The next morning, I rushed into the garden. Tiny green shoots had pushed through the soil, stretching towards the sunlight. They looked delicate, yet determined.

As the weeks passed, the seedlings grew into tall sunflowers that brightened the entire garden. Neighbours stopped to admire them, children clicked photographs, and many asked for seeds to plant in their own homes.

Looking at the golden flowers swaying in the breeze, I realised that hope, like a seed, often lies hidden beneath the surface. It blossoms only when nourished with faith, care, and action. If humanity wishes for a greener future, we must first prove that we deserve the trust of the seeds we plant.

## A Day Inside Other People's Minds

It was a quiet Sunday evening when I noticed a gentle glow beneath the old banyan tree near my house. Standing there was a radiant angel with kind eyes and a warm smile.

"You have always wondered why people behave the way they do," the angel said. "For one day, I shall give you the power to hear their thoughts. But remember, understanding someone's mind is a privilege, not a game."

Before I could reply, the angel touched my forehead and disappeared.

The next morning, I visited the grocery shop. The shopkeeper smiled politely as he served his customers. Suddenly, I heard his thoughts.

*"I hope I earn enough today to pay for my daughter's school fees. She must never give up her education because of money."*

His cheerful face had hidden a mountain of worries. I realised that behind every smile may lie a silent struggle.

Later, I went to a nearby park. On a lonely bench sat a young man staring at the lake. To everyone else, he appeared calm. But his thoughts reached me clearly.

*"I miss my parents. Ever since I moved to this city for work, I have not had a real conversation with anyone. I wish someone would simply ask me how I am."*

I walked over, introduced myself, and spent a few minutes talking to him. As we spoke, his face slowly brightened. Sometimes, the greatest gift we can offer another person is our time and attention.

In the evening, I met my neighbour, Rohan, a mischievous boy who was often scolded for playing pranks. I expected to hear plans for his next trick. Instead, I heard something unexpected.

*"If I make everyone laugh, perhaps they won't notice how lonely I feel after my parents leave for work every day."*

I was speechless. What I had mistaken for naughtiness was, in truth, a child's quiet cry for affection.

As the sun began to set, the angel appeared once more.

"Well," the angel asked, "would you like this power forever?"

I smiled and shook my head.

"No," I replied. "Today has taught me something far more valuable. I do not need to read minds if I learn to listen with patience, observe with care, and treat every person with kindness."

The angel nodded and vanished into the evening sky.

Since that day, I have often remembered those silent thoughts. They taught me that every person is fighting a battle we cannot see. A little empathy, a kind word, or a listening ear can sometimes heal wounds that remain invisible to the world.
