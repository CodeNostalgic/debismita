Model Essay

The Forgotten Station

Structure Highlights
Topic Type: Argumentative | Word Count: 396 Words | Reading Time: ~3 mins | Standard: ICSE Board Exam
Introduction

"They said the train hadn't stopped at that station in decades—until it did, just as I arrived."

Body Paragraph 1

I looked around in disbelief. The tiny platform was deserted except for an old station building with faded walls and a rusted signboard that read Shantipur Halt. Wild grass had grown between the railway tracks, and the silence was broken only by the chirping of birds.

Body Paragraph 2

I had been travelling to my cousin's village when heavy rain forced our bus to take a diversion. While searching for shelter, I had unknowingly wandered to this abandoned station. Just as I wondered how I would continue my journey, the distant whistle of a train echoed through the valley.

Body Paragraph 3

An elderly man sitting on a wooden bench smiled mysteriously. "It hasn't stopped here for nearly forty years," he said, "but today seems different."

Body Paragraph 4

To my astonishment, the train slowed down and came to a complete halt. The guard stepped out and announced that a fallen tree further along the track had forced the train to stop temporarily until the line was cleared.

Body Paragraph 5

While the passengers stretched their legs, I noticed a weathered photograph hanging inside the old stationmaster's office. It showed a crowded platform decorated with flowers and flags. Curious, I asked the elderly man about it.

Body Paragraph 6

He sighed and began his story. Years ago, Shantipur Halt had been a busy station that connected several villages. However, after a new railway line was built, trains stopped coming, and the station was gradually abandoned. "This place once echoed with laughter, farewells, and joyful reunions," he said softly. "Now, only memories remain."

Body Paragraph 7

Listening to him, I realised how easily time can erase places that were once full of life.

Body Paragraph 8

An hour later, the track was cleared, and the train was ready to leave. Before boarding, I turned to thank the old man, but the bench was empty. A railway worker nearby smiled and said, "People often say an old stationmaster loved this place so much that he never truly left it."

Conclusion

As the train pulled away, I looked back at the lonely platform growing smaller in the distance. Whether the old man had been real or merely a keeper of forgotten memories, I could not say. But I knew one thing for certain: some places may disappear from maps, yet they continue to live in the stories and hearts of those who remember them.

Vocabulary Builder
Word Meaning Alternative (Synonym)
Deserted Empty of people Abandoned, uninhabited
Diversion An alternative route for use when a usual road is closed Detour, deviation
Astonishment Great surprise Amazement, wonder
Weathered Worn by long exposure to the atmosphere Faded, worn
