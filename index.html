PART I — REFERENCE TO CONTEXT (RTC)
Extract 1
SHYLOCK:
“I hate him for he is a Christian,
But more for that in low simplicity
He lends out money gratis and brings down
The rate of usance here in Venice.
If I can catch him once upon the hip,
I will feed fat the ancient grudge I bear him.”
________________________________________
Questions & Answers
a) Who is speaking these lines and to whom?
Answer:
Shylock speaks these lines in an aside. He is speaking directly to the audience about Antonio.
________________________________________
b) Why does Shylock hate Antonio? Give two reasons.
Answer:
Shylock hates Antonio because:
1.	Antonio is a Christian, and Shylock feels religious hostility towards Christians.
2.	Antonio lends money without charging interest, which damages Shylock’s moneylending business.
________________________________________
c) Explain the following phrases:
“catch him once upon the hip”
This is a wrestling expression meaning to gain an advantage over someone or trap them in a helpless situation.
“feed fat the ancient grudge”
This means to strengthen and satisfy his long-standing hatred and desire for revenge.
________________________________________
d) What does this extract reveal about Shylock’s character?
Answer:
The extract reveals Shylock as:
•	Revengeful
•	Bitter
•	Calculating
•	Deeply prejudiced
•	Economically competitive
It also shows that he carefully hides his hatred beneath polite business behaviour.
________________________________________
Deeper Analysis
This extract is extremely important because it exposes Shylock’s true motive behind the bond. While he outwardly behaves like a businessman, inwardly he is driven by resentment and revenge.
The phrase “ancient grudge” suggests that the hatred between Antonio and Shylock has existed for a long time. Shakespeare uses this moment to establish the emotional foundation for the later trial scene.

Extract 2
ANTONIO:
“The devil can cite Scripture for his purpose!
An evil soul producing holy witness
Is like a villain with a smiling cheek,
A goodly apple rotten at the heart.”
________________________________________
Questions & Answers
a) On what occasion does Antonio say these lines?
Answer:
Antonio says these lines after Shylock uses the Biblical story of Jacob and Laban to justify charging interest on loans.
________________________________________
b) What literary devices are used in these lines?
Answer:
The following literary devices are used:
•	Metaphor
•	Simile
•	Biblical Allusion
•	Imagery
________________________________________
c) What does this reveal about Antonio’s attitude towards Shylock?
Answer:
Antonio believes that Shylock is hypocritical and dishonest. He thinks Shylock misuses religion to justify greed.
________________________________________
d) Explain the dramatic irony in this extract.
Answer:
Antonio believes that he understands Shylock completely, yet he still agrees to the dangerous bond. The audience already senses that Shylock’s kindness is false.
________________________________________
Deeper Analysis
The comparison of Shylock to “a goodly apple rotten at the heart” reflects the theme of appearance versus reality. Shakespeare repeatedly shows characters who appear kind or noble externally but hide dangerous motives internally.
Antonio’s statement also reflects the religious prejudice of the time, where Jews were often viewed suspiciously by Christians.

Extract 3
SHYLOCK:
“This is kind I offer.”
BASSANIO:
“This were kindness!”
SHYLOCK:
“Go with me to a notary, seal me there
Your single bond; and, in a merry sport,
If you repay me not on such a day...
let the forfeit
Be nominated for an equal pound
Of your fair flesh...”

Questions & Answers
a) Why does Shylock call the bond a “merry sport”?
Answer:
Shylock pretends that the condition is only a harmless joke meant in fun. However, he secretly intends to use it for revenge.
________________________________________
b) Why does Bassanio object to the bond?
Answer:
Bassanio instinctively distrusts Shylock and senses danger in the unusual condition.
________________________________________

Answer:
Antonio is extremely confident that his ships will return safely before the bond expires.
________________________________________

This extract introduces the famous “pound of flesh” bond, which later becomes the central conflict of the play.

PART II — SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS
Q1. Why does Shylock call the bond a “merry bond”?
Answer:
Shylock calls it a “merry bond” to make it appear harmless and humorous. In reality, it is a carefully designed trap intended to satisfy his desire for revenge against Antonio.
________________________________________
Q2. How does Shylock justify charging interest on loans?
Answer:
Shylock narrates the Biblical story of Jacob and Laban. He argues that Jacob prospered through intelligence and divine blessing. Therefore, earning profit through moneylending is not sinful.
________________________________________
Q3. Why does Bassanio object to the bond while Antonio agrees?
Answer:
Bassanio is cautious and suspicious of Shylock’s motives. Antonio, however, is overconfident about the return of his ships and dismisses all danger.
________________________________________
Q4. What does Shylock mean by “suff’rance is the badge of all our tribe”?
Answer:
Shylock means that Jews are accustomed to silently enduring insult, humiliation, and persecution.

PART III — LONG ANSWER / ESSAY QUESTIONS
Q1. Describe Shylock’s attitude towards Antonio in this scene.
Answer
Shylock’s attitude towards Antonio is filled with deep hatred, resentment, and a desire for revenge. His hatred arises from several reasons.
First, there is religious hostility. Shylock openly admits that he hates Antonio because he is a Christian. The conflict between Christians and Jews is one of the major themes of the play.
Second, Antonio harms Shylock economically by lending money without interest. This reduces the rate of usury in Venice and damages Shylock’s profession as a moneylender.
Third, Antonio has repeatedly humiliated Shylock in public. He has insulted him, called him names such as “dog,” spat upon him, and criticised his business practices openly on the Rialto.
Though Shylock behaves politely outwardly, he secretly plans revenge. His statement “If I can catch him once upon the hip” reveals his determination to trap Antonio legally through the bond.
Thus, Shylock’s attitude combines wounded pride, religious bitterness, and calculated revenge.
________________________________________
Q2. Bring out the contrast between Antonio and Shylock.
Answer
Aspect	Antonio	Shylock
Religion	Christian	Jew
Attitude to Money	Lends without interest	Charges interest
Personality	Generous and open	Cautious and calculating
Behaviour	Proud and blunt	Polite but revengeful
Motive	Friendship	Profit and revenge
Language	Direct	Sarcastic and clever
Antonio represents Christian charity and generosity, while Shylock represents the practical world of business and legal justice.
However, Shakespeare does not present either character as completely good or evil. Antonio is generous but prejudiced. Shylock is cruel yet also deeply wronged.
________________________________________
Q3. How does this scene prepare the ground for future conflict?
Answer
This scene lays the foundation for the major conflict of the play.
The “pound of flesh” bond becomes the central issue of the later trial scene. Antonio’s confidence creates dramatic irony because the audience suspects that disaster may occur.
The scene also develops several important themes:
•	Revenge versus mercy
•	Religious prejudice
•	Friendship and sacrifice
•	Risk and uncertainty
•	Appearance versus reality
Shylock’s hidden motives and Antonio’s overconfidence together create tension and suspense.

PART IV — CHARACTER SKETCHES
Character Sketch of Shylock
Answer
In Act 1, Scene 3, Shylock emerges as one of Shakespeare’s most complex characters.
He is shown as revengeful and deeply bitter. He hates Antonio for religious, economic, and personal reasons. His desire to “feed fat the ancient grudge” reveals his determination to take revenge.
Shylock is also intelligent and cunning. He cleverly disguises the dangerous bond as a “merry sport.” He speaks politely while secretly planning Antonio’s destruction.
At the same time, Shakespeare presents Shylock as a victim of prejudice. Antonio has publicly humiliated him by insulting, spitting upon, and mocking him because he is Jewish.
His line “suff’rance is the badge of all our tribe” evokes sympathy because it reflects the suffering endured by Jews in Venetian society.
Shylock is also practical and business-minded. His repeated focus on “three thousand ducats” shows his careful financial thinking.
Thus, Shylock appears both frightening and pitiable — a man shaped by years of hatred and humiliation.
________________________________________
Character Sketch of Antonio
Answer
Antonio appears as a generous and loyal friend who is willing to risk his life to help Bassanio.
His greatest quality is friendship. He agrees to become responsible for Bassanio’s loan without hesitation.
Antonio is also proud and fearless. Even when borrowing money from Shylock, he refuses to hide his dislike and openly admits he may insult Shylock again.
However, Antonio’s tragic flaw is overconfidence. He believes too strongly in the safe return of his ships and underestimates the danger of the bond.
Antonio also reflects the Christian prejudice of Venetian society. His harsh treatment of Shylock reveals intolerance and arrogance.
Therefore, Antonio is noble and admirable in some ways, yet flawed in others.

PART V — THEMES IN THE SCENE
1. Revenge
Shylock’s “ancient grudge” drives the entire conflict of the scene. The bond is not merely financial; it is personal revenge disguised as legal agreement.
________________________________________
2. Prejudice and Antisemitism
The scene clearly reveals mutual hatred between Christians and Jews.
•	Antonio insults Shylock publicly.
•	Shylock resents Christians deeply.
•	Religious divisions influence every interaction.
________________________________________
3. Usury versus Charity
Antonio believes lending money without interest is morally correct.
Shylock believes earning profit through moneylending is practical and justified.
This creates both economic and moral conflict.
________________________________________
4. Appearance versus Reality
Shylock’s “kindness” hides dangerous intentions.
The “merry bond” appears harmless but becomes deadly later.
________________________________________
5. Fate and Risk
Antonio’s fortune depends on ships travelling across dangerous seas.
This uncertainty creates suspense and dramatic tension.
